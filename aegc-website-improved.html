About AEGC

The regulator
before regulation.

AEGC operates as an independent, council-governed authority — establishing binding ethical standards for AI systems before governments can legislate and after the industry fails to self-regulate.

Who We Are

Independent by
Design

The AI Ethics & Governance Council is structured to be uninfluenceable. No single corporation, government, or research body controls more than one seat on the governing council. Decisions are made by supermajority vote across 28 members drawn from academia, civil society, law, and technical AI research.

This independence is our foundation. When AEGC certifies a model, it means something — because AEGC cannot be bought, pressured, or captured by the systems it governs.

01

Human-in-the-Loop Governance

Every certification decision involves direct human review. No automated approval pathway exists for any tier.

02

Continuous Monitoring Mandate

Certification is never a one-time event. Certified models are monitored continuously against evolving ethical benchmarks.

03

Risk-Proportional Accountability

Higher-impact AI systems face more rigorous governance requirements. Accountability scales with deployment risk.

04

Transparent Voting Record

All council votes and the reasoning behind certification decisions are published in the Open Ethics Registry.

Technology

Architecture
built for
accountability.

AEGC's Council-Governed Multimodal Intelligence Architecture is the first AI governance stack designed from the ground up around human oversight — not bolted on after the fact.

Three-Layer Architecture

How It Works

Each layer is independently auditable, replaceable, and governed. There is no single point of ethical failure.

01

Core AI Layer

The foundation multimodal model handling input processing across text, image, audio, and structured data. The core layer operates without access to governance parameters — ensuring that capability and ethics are developed independently.

  • Multimodal input/output processing
  • Capability-focused training pipeline
  • Full audit trail of model operations
02

Council Governance Layer

The governance layer sits between capability and deployment. Human council members review, constrain, and override AI behavior through a structured voting and veto mechanism. Every governance decision is cryptographically signed and immutable.

  • Human-in-the-loop enforcement
  • Bias & harm detection pipeline
  • Signed governance vote ledger
03

Memory & Safety Layer

Long-term alignment memory stores the history of all model behaviors, governance interventions, and incident resolutions. Automated rollback protocols can instantly revert a certified model to a previous safe state if a violation is detected.

  • Immutable audit log (append-only)
  • Automated rollback protocols
  • Long-term alignment memory store
